assistant: # The Eisenhower Method for Task Prioritization

The **Eisenhower Method** (also called the **Eisenhower Matrix** or **Urgent-Important Matrix**) is a productivity framework attributed to Dwight D. Eisenhower (34th U.S. President and 5-star Army General). It helps you prioritize tasks by urgency and importance.

## The Four Quadrants

| | **Urgent** | **Not Urgent** |
|----------------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Important** | **Q1: Do Immediately** | **Q2: Schedule** |
| **Not Important** | **Q3: Delegate** | **Q4: Eliminate** |

### What Each Quadrant Means:

**🔴 Q1 - Do Immediately (Urgent & Important)**
- Crises, deadlines, emergencies
- Pay these things first
- Examples: client deliverables due today, critical bugs, important meetings

**🟡 Q2 - Schedule (Not Urgent & Important)**
- Strategic planning, relationship building, personal development
- Invest time here for long-term success
- Examples: exercise, skill building, strategic planning

**🟢 Q3 - Delegate (Urgent & Not Important)**
- Interruptions, some emails, some calls
- Can often be delegated or minimized
- Examples: scheduling meetings, routine reports

**⚪ Q4 - Eliminate (Not Urgent & Not Important)**
- Time wasters, trivial activities
- Avoid or minimize these
- Examples: excessive social media, aimless browsing

## How to Use It

1. **List all tasks** you need to complete
2. **Categorize each** into one of four quadrants
3. **Act accordingly** based on quadrant
4. **Review regularly** (daily/weekly)

## Key Insight

> The most productive people **spend more time in Q2** (important but not urgent), because they're investing in prevention and planning, not just putting out fires.
